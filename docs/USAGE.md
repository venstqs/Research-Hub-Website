# Usage & Demonstration Guide

This guide outlines how to interact with the School Research Hub.

## 1. Homepage & Carousels
Upon loading the site, you are greeted by the **Hero Banner**, which spotlights a highly-rated or award-winning paper. 
- Below the banner, research papers are categorized into horizontal carousels (e.g., "Trending This Week", "Robotics & Embedded Systems").
- Use your mouse or trackpad to scroll horizontally, or use the navigation arrows that appear when hovering over the edges of a row.

## 2. Real-Time Search
- Locate the **Search Bar** in the top navigation menu.
- Start typing a keyword, author name, or title (e.g., "AI", "Bioplastic").
- The interface will instantly transition from horizontal rows to a grid layout displaying the relevant search results.
- Clear the search using the "X" icon to return to the default view.

## 3. Quick-View Modal
- Click on any research card to open the **Quick-View Modal**.
- This sleek overlay displays:
  - Full title and authors.
  - Strand, Year, and specific Award badges.
  - The full abstract.
  - Keyword tags.
- From here, you can click **"Read Full PDF"** (which opens the associated document link, e.g., Google Drive) or interact with the Offline saving feature.

## 4. Offline Capabilities
A core feature of the Hub is its ability to function without an internet connection, relying on the browser's local cache.

### Saving a Paper
- Open a paper's Quick-View Modal.
- Click **"Save for Offline Access"**. The button will change to indicate it is saved.

### Viewing Saved Papers
- Click the **"Offline Saved"** tab in the navigation bar. Here, you will see a grid of all papers you have downloaded to your local cache.

### Simulating Offline Mode
To test the offline functionality:
- Click the **"Live Mode"** toggle in the navigation bar. It will switch to a red **"Offline Simulated"** state.
- A warning banner will appear indicating you are browsing local cache only.
- Navigate to the Home or Strands tab. You will notice that *only* the papers you previously saved are visible, proving that the application successfully filters out non-cached data when disconnected.
